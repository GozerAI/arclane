# Arclane 90-Day User Journey

> **The Arclane Promise:** From idea to investor-ready business in 90 nights. Every morning you wake up to new strategy, research, assets, and growth tools — built overnight by AI, guided by your vision.

---

## 1. Program Overview

| Phase | Days | Goal | Key Deliverables | Credits Used | Minimum Plan |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | 1–21 | Validate the idea, establish presence | Strategy brief, market research, landing page, growth assets | 12 credits | Starter (10) + bonus |
| **Validation** | 22–45 | Prove demand, build pitch capability | Validation plan, outreach templates, pitch deck v1, KPI tracker | 9 credits | Pro (20) recommended |
| **Growth** | 46–75 | Acquire customers, build ops | Acquisition playbook, email sequences, revenue tracking | 11 credits | Growth (75) recommended |
| **Scale-Ready** | 76–90 | Prepare for investment or scale | Investor deck, hiring plan, 90-day report, graduation assessment | 8 credits | Pro or Growth |
| **Forever Partner** | 91+ | Adaptive, compounding growth partnership | Ongoing nightly tasks, weekly digest, revenue attribution | 1–5/week | Any paid plan |

> **Credit economics:** The initial 4-deliverable cycle uses 1 bonus credit (included on all plans). Each subsequent overnight run costs 1 credit. The full 90-day program consumes approximately 40 credits across all phases — making Growth ($249/mo, 75 credits) the most cost-efficient plan for completing the program in a single billing period.

## 4. Graduation Criteria Per Phase

### Phase 1 → Phase 2
- Landing page live and receiving traffic
- At least 1 competitor profile complete
- Financial model baseline established
- Strategy brief reviewed and accepted by founder

### Phase 2 → Phase 3
- At least 1 validation hypothesis tested (outreach sent or ads run)
- Pitch deck v1 complete
- KPI tracker configured
- Funnel baseline measured

### Phase 3 → Phase 4
- At least 1 acquisition channel active
- Email sequence deployed
- Revenue tracking instrumented
- At least 2 growth experiments logged

### Phase 4 → Forever Partner (Day 91)
- 90-day report generated
- Graduation assessment health score ≥ 60
- Q2 plan accepted
- Forever Partner mode activated in dashboard

## 7. User Experience Narrative

**Day 1 — The intake form is short. Deceptively short.**

A founder signs up, answers 8 questions about their idea, and logs off. The next morning, their dashboard shows a Core Strategy Brief, a Market Research Report, a live landing page at `[slug].arclane.cloud`, and a Growth Asset Bundle. This is the moment Arclane hooks them. The work is real. It's not templates — it's tailored to their specific market, their specific offer.

**Week 2 — The rhythm sets in.**

They start waking up to check their dashboard before coffee. Competitor profiles arrive. Their offer gets sharper. The AI noticed their landing page headline was positioning them against enterprise software; the offer refinement doc explains why they should reframe for SMBs instead. They didn't ask for that insight — it just arrived.

**Week 3 — The first inflection point.**

The financial model lands. For most founders, this is the first time they've ever seen a break-even projection with their actual numbers. The SEO baseline shows 3 keywords they hadn't considered — low competition, high intent. They start to feel like they have a real business, not just an idea.

**Day 22 — Phase 2 begins and the tone shifts.**

Foundation is about building. Validation is about proving. The AI delivers a Validation Plan with 3 testable hypotheses and clear success criteria. Outreach templates are in the dashboard, ready to copy into their email client. For the first time, the founder is going out to talk to real customers — and they're doing it with professional-grade tools they didn't have to write.

**Week 6 — The pitch deck arrives.**

Twelve slides. Clean. Investor-framing. When they open the pitch deck for the first time, many founders describe feeling a jolt of credibility they hadn't expected. This is a real deck. It references their actual market size, their actual traction, their actual ask. They share it with a friend who's an angel investor. The friend asks for a follow-up call.

**Week 8 — Growth phase momentum.**

The acquisition playbook is the first deliverable that feels like a genuine operational plan. Not a strategy brief — a week-by-week tactical guide for three specific channels. The 7-email onboarding sequence is written and ready to load into their email tool. The founder is no longer building — they're executing. Arclane shifted from architect to operator without any instruction.

**Week 12 — Scale reality check.**

The Scale Assessment Report arrives with three colored indicators: green for market readiness, amber for team, red for operations. It's honest. The hiring plan is specific — not generic titles but actual scoped roles for their stage and budget. The founder realizes they've been thinking about the wrong first hire.

**Day 89 — The 90-Day Report.**

Every deliverable, every milestone, every gap. The AI has been tracking the whole time. The report shows what worked, what was skipped, and what to prioritize in Q2. The Graduation Assessment produces a health score. The founder's is 74 — above average for their cohort. Forever Partner mode activates automatically.

**Day 91 — Something new begins.**

The AI doesn't stop. It adapts. Instead of a fixed program, it scans the business health scores each night and picks the highest-impact task automatically. Some nights that's a new content batch. Some nights it's a competitive monitoring alert. Some nights it surfaces a financial anomaly. The founder no longer has to manage the backlog — the system manages itself.
